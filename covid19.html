Due to CoViD-19, Marden High School has updated the school policies.

You can find these rules on this document.


Home Testing:

Students are rquired to take a test Monday and Thursday morning (or the night before).
For more information, please visit https://www.mardenhigh.net/rapid-testing-of-students-at-home/.
You can report results using this google form.

Year Bubbles:

  • To ensure all students stay safe, students will be in “year bubbles” during social times, including before school, break times, and lunch times.

Before school bubbles:

School will not open before 8:30.




Break and lunch bubbles:

You must stay in your bubble zone, unless going to the toilet.

Distancing:

  • Students are advised to keep a 'respectful distance' from eachother.

Face Coverings:

  • The new rules state that face coverings now only have to be worn in corridoors, where year bubbles can't be sperated.
    This means that if students wish, they can take them off in their bubble zone (e.g- classrooms).
  • You may take your mask off to eat inside.
  • Face coverings must also be worn when queuing at food outlets.
  • Face coverings must be worn over the mouth and nose when they are on, and not worn covering the chin.

Cross contamination:

  • Students must bring their own stationary and equipment.
  • Students are not allowed to share their equipment with other students to reduce cross-contamination.
  • Students must sanitise their hands when their enter and leave a classroom, at the start and end of breaks, and after going to the toilet.
    They are encouraged to sanitse as traveling around school.
  • When a student needs to go to the toilet, they must wash their hands for at least 20 seconds.
  • Students are not allowed to gather with friends in the toilets and when going to their locker.
  • There are new pedal bins around school, which can be operated with a foot.
  • Students are not allowed to hug, high five, or shake hands.
  • Students have set seating plans in their classes. This means that students must not move around in a class mid-way through a (half) term. This is to help with track and trace.

Cleaning:

  • Cleaning routines have been increased.

Further Changes:

  • Students must wear their PE kit on the days that they have PE.
  • When students arrive at a lesson, they must go straight into the classroom:
    There is no need to wait in the corridor, except for outside science labs and technology rooms, unless there is already a teacher in the classroom.
  • Students must go home promptly, and not stay at the space in front of the gate.
  • To increase air circulation, windows have to be open in classrooms.
    This means that students have now been given the choice to wear a coat in the classroom if they are cold.

Detention Changes:

  • Detentions will be heald the day after a student had recieved the detention.
  • Detentions will last for 15 minutes, from 3:15 to 3:30.
  • Detentions will be held with the student's head of year.
  • These detentions can be for any reason, incuding behavior, lateness, and homework.
  • The member of staff who teaches the student period 5 will walk them to their head of year for their detention.
  • The time spent will be restorative, discussing 1:1 how best to avoid similar sanctions in future.
  • A text message will go out to parents / carers on the day the detention is set so they are aware of the detention the following day.
