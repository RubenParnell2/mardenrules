Marden High School Rules: Mobile Devices and Online Saftey



As stated in the The Student Handbook, page 2, point 29, students must follow rules about mobile devices.

Rules about Mobile Devives

As stated on page 10 of The Student Handbook:
  • Students can bring phones to school, but they should be switched off.
  • Students using them between 8:45 and 3:15 will have their confiscated them until the end of the day.
  • If students need to contact their parents or guardians, they should go to reception.
  • If parents or guardians need to contact a student, they should ring reception on 0191 296 2771.
As stated in the Year Seven Online Open Evening, Sept 2020, question 'Can my child bring a phone into school?':
  • 'Occationally, mobile devices may be used for a specific educational purpose, as directed by class teachers.'

Online Saftey
Students using social media should ensure that they are the correct age to be using the service.
Apps like Snapchat, Instagram, etc. have an age restriction of 13+. This means that students in year 7 should not use these apps.

These apps pose a risk for students to be cyber-bullied, so it is vital that students know the risks of using these apps.

People should not:
  • Be unkind or disrespectful to others (either directly to them or about them) - the same social rules apply online as in person.
  • Create large group chats without the consent of those that you are inviting.
  • Screenshot messages or social media content (videos, images) created by others to ridicule or be unkind about them.
  • Connect with people you don’t know.
  • Share other students’ details (phone number, social media profiles) with others.
  • Be complicit or silent about negative social media interactions that you witness - by doing this you are allowing these negative behaviours to continue.
